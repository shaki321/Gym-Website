"Nutrition is a critical part of health and development". Significant studies and evidence show that a balanced nutritional diet provides improved infant, child and maternal health, stronger imumne system, safer pregnancy and childbirth, lower risk of communicable diseases (such as diabetes and cardivascular diseases), and most importantly longevity.

Children with a balanced diet of healthy foods learn better. Sufficient nutrition allow people to be more productive both mentally and physically.

A lack of nutrition (called malnutrition), presents a threat to human health. Today's world faces two global problems; malnutrition in low and middle income countries and overeating in high income countries.

As countries continue to grow, the demand of food continue to rise. The convience of fast food, takeaway food and processed food.
